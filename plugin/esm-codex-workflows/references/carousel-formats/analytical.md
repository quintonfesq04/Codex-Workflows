# Analytical Carousel Format

Use this preset for stat cases, player debates, awards arguments, value comparisons, and posts where the point is "here is the evidence."

## Trigger Examples

- `Format: analytical`
- `Make an analytical carousel about Cristopher Sanchez`
- `Build a Cy Young case carousel`
- `Make a stat proof carousel for Bobby Witt Jr.`

## Best For

- One-player or one-team argument.
- Awards cases.
- Trade-value analysis when the post is more proof than rumor.
- "People are missing this" stat posts.

## Default Slide Map

1. Cover: short debate or claim, no subtext.
2. Context: what changed or why this matters now.
3. Main proof: strongest stat cluster.
4. Support proof: workload, percentile, split, leaderboard, or trend.
5. Tension/counterpoint: what could still make the argument messy.
6. Verdict or meme: final takeaway, or a reaction meme when the account style calls for one.

## Aesthetic Direction

- Clean, serious, stat-first.
- Cover should feel big and authoritative, with either deep team-color gradient or black gradient plus one team-color hook word.
- Info slides should use a tight white copy band and a strong image below.
- Bottom stat should be the visual payoff, not a small footnote.
- Avoid clutter, extra labels, decorative shapes, and too many numbers in the copy band.

## Required Inputs

- Team and exact hex colors.
- Cover text.
- One cover image and one image per info slide when possible.
- Final copy and bottom stat for each slide, or a clear research request.
- Meme still only if the brief includes a meme slide.

## Verification

- The argument is easy to understand from the slide order alone.
- Every info slide has one main job.
- Bottom stat matches the copy and is readable at thumbnail size.
- Gradients on info slides are softer than cover gradients.
- No supplied stats are re-verified unless the user asks for research.
