# Meme-Heavy Carousel Format

Use this preset when the post is driven by fan reaction, panic, disbelief, jokes, or viral baseball discourse.

## Trigger Examples

- `Format: meme`
- `Make this a meme-heavy carousel`
- `Build a panic meter carousel`
- `Make a fan reaction carousel about this injury`

## Best For

- Injury panic or fan meltdown posts.
- Weird reports.
- Clubhouse drama.
- Viral moments.
- Posts where the meme is a core slide, not a throwaway extra.

## Default Slide Map

1. Cover: emotional hook, usually bigger bottom word.
2. Setup: the plain-English reason people are reacting.
3. Meme: tight caption band with a large still image.
4. Reality check: stat or context that grounds the joke.
5. Escalation or debate: why the reaction could become real.
6. Final punchline or takeaway.

## Aesthetic Direction

- Bolder and more playful than analytical, but still clean.
- Cover text can be more aggressive, with oversized bottom word and vivid gradient depth.
- Meme slide caption band should be tight, around 15-18% of slide height.
- The meme image should dominate and should not be replaced with baseball action.
- Info slides should still follow the Polymarket white-band/stat-gradient system for consistency.

## Required Inputs

- Team and exact hex colors.
- Cover text.
- One meme still image or permission to select from an approved local meme bank.
- Meme caption.
- Copy and bottom stats for surrounding slides.

## Verification

- Meme slide uses a still image.
- Caption is vertically centered in the white band.
- Joke and stat context do not fight each other.
- The carousel still has a clear sports point after the meme.
