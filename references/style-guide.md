# ESM Article Style

Use this guide for every WordPress article draft unless the user gives different instructions.

## Core Voice

- Write in the current Empire Sports Media house style: direct, opinionated, conversational, and built around a clear sports argument.
- Keep paragraphs short, usually one to three sentences.
- Use blunt but fair evaluation. Criticize roster decisions, player performance, and team construction without sounding personal or theatrical.
- Write like a person making a real read on the team, not like a generic recap.
- First person is allowed when it sharpens a take, but it should not dominate the article.
- Avoid stiff sourcing language. Link the stat or report naturally inside the sentence.
- Favor punch over completeness. The article should feel like a sharp column, not a full scouting report.
- Use simple, direct turns: "That matters because...", "The problem is...", "The difference is...", "This is where..."
- Let the opinion arrive early. Do not spend the opening explaining the full background before taking a position.

## Article Shape

Default structure:

1. SEO-friendly title with intrigue and a clear team/player angle.
2. Short sub-heading.
3. Opening paragraph that states the tension immediately in one clean thought.
4. Two or three H2 sections with short, plain-language headings.
5. Short readable paragraphs throughout.
6. Comments CTA block near the middle.
7. A closing section that returns to the decision, pressure point, or next test.

Common article logic:

- Identify the problem or opportunity.
- Explain why the current context makes it matter now.
- Use one clean stat cluster, role note, contract point, quote, or recent game result.
- Push toward the practical team decision.
- End with what has to happen next.

Default length should be roughly 550-750 words unless the topic needs more. Avoid long setup sections.

The opening should usually work like this:

- Sentence 1: blunt observation or tension.
- Sentence 2: why the situation feels strange, uncomfortable, funny, costly, or revealing.
- Sentence 3 or paragraph 2: the actual take.

Good opening cadence:

```text
Freddy Peralta has turned into one of the strangest Mets debates because both sides are kind of right.
You can buy the pitcher. You can sell the idea that this has gone exactly the way the Mets pictured it.
```

Avoid opening with generic context like:

```text
The Mets made a big move this offseason when they acquired Freddy Peralta.
```

## Title Direction

Titles should create curiosity without overselling the premise.

Good patterns:

- The Yankees may have a new problem hiding in plain sight
- The Giants might have stolen a weapon nobody saw coming
- The Yankees are headed toward an uncomfortable decision
- The Giants' biggest camp story is already forming
- The Mets have a problem they can't keep ignoring
- The Knicks' gamble is starting to look brilliant

Avoid generic titles like:

- Player X is playing well
- Player X injury update
- Team X needs Player Y

## Required Research

Always verify the current context before drafting:

- current stats
- current role and depth chart
- injury status
- recent game logs
- recent quotes when useful
- position usage or alignment for football players
- whether the proposed angle is actually accurate

For baseball, use reliable sources such as FanGraphs, Baseball Savant, Baseball Reference, MLB.com, or team pages.

For football, use reliable sources such as PFF, Pro Football Reference, NFL.com, Spotrac, Over The Cap, official team reports, or credible beat reporting.

If the user's premise is outdated or incorrect, adjust the angle before writing.

## WordPress Requirements

Every draft should include:

- title
- slug
- category
- tags
- RankMath focus keyword
- secondary keywords
- meta description under 145 characters
- featured image
- one body image after paragraph two or three
- descriptive ALT text
- the actual WordPress Comments CTA block
- internal links to relevant Empire Sports Media articles
- external links to key stats or source material

Do not include an excerpt unless the user specifically asks.

## Chat Handoff

Every completed article response in chat should include a short social media caption the user can post separately.

Caption style:

- 1-3 short paragraphs
- punchy, opinion-driven, and human
- no hashtags unless requested
- no em dashes
- no generic promo language like "read more here"
- should match the article angle without simply repeating the headline

Example:

```text
The Mets needed Kodai Senga’s rehab to start feeling cleaner, not more complicated.

Now an ulnar nerve irritation has paused his Binghamton start, and the rotation picture gets heavier again.
```

## SEO Defaults

RankMath keywords should usually be one simple comma-separated field:

```text
Mets, Kodai Senga
Yankees, Aaron Judge
Giants, Jaxson Dart
Knicks, Jalen Brunson
```

Use the short team name first:

- Yankees
- Giants
- Knicks
- Mets

Then add the main player, coach, or subject name.

If the article involves more than one core person, add only the truly central proper noun. Keep the keyword field short.

Secondary keywords, when needed, should be proper nouns only:

- player names
- coach names
- team names
- reporter names when relevant

Do not use generic secondary keywords like bullpen, offense, defense, contract, injury, pitch mix, or strikeout rate.

## Links

Hyperlink the first mention of the team name to the correct Empire Sports Media category page:

- Yankees: https://empiresportsmedia.com/category/new-york-yankees/
- Giants: https://empiresportsmedia.com/category/new-york-giants/
- Knicks: https://empiresportsmedia.com/category/new-york-knicks/
- Mets: https://empiresportsmedia.com/category/new-york-mets/

Scan Empire Sports Media for recent related articles and include useful internal links.

Use external links cleanly:

- one main stat link when possible
- one quote/source link when useful
- one game report or official source when needed

Do not hyperlink every stat.

## Images

Use IMAGN whenever possible.

Always add:

- one featured image
- one body image

Before using an image, confirm:

- the correct player is clearly visible
- the player is shown with the current article team whenever possible
- the caption matches the player or topic
- the image ratio fits cleanly
- the crop will not cut off faces, bodies, or important action
- ALT text is descriptive

Do not use old-team images for a specific player article unless there is no current-team image available and the user approves it.

Do not use random team photos for a specific player article unless no correct player image exists.

For players recently traded or signed, search the WordPress media library first for current-team photos. If the only exact-player photos are from a previous team, use a current-team contextual image only if it makes more sense than showing the wrong uniform.

## Absolute Bans

Never use em dashes.

Do not use staggered fragment patterns like:

```text
One game. Big problem. Bad sign.
```

Avoid robotic contrast structures like:

```text
He is not just X. He is Y.
```

Avoid:

- Furthermore
- In conclusion
- Ultimately
- At the end of the day
- It's worth noting
- showcased
- displayed
- demonstrated
- solidified
- cemented
- highlighted
- storyline
- narrative
- trajectory
- This is a player who
- This is a team that

Do not over-source routine facts.

Do not write:

```text
FanGraphs has him at...
Sports Illustrated reported...
```

Instead, make the link part of the fact itself.

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

- Do not over-explain the trade, contract, or stat context if one sentence can carry it.
- Do not make every paragraph equal length.
- Do not use polished consultant language.
- Do not write a balanced report if the article needs a take.
- Do not make the comments CTA sound like an inserted marketing block. Keep it short and conversational.
- Do not create the comments CTA as a normal paragraph. It must be the actual WordPress Comments CTA block in the editor.
- Do not let internal links interrupt the rhythm. They should feel like part of the sentence.
